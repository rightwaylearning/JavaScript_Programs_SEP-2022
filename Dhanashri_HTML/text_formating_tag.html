Hunman in Ramayana

The Rāmāyana (/rɑːˈmɑːjənə/;[1][2] Sanskrit: रामायणम्,[3] IAST: Rāmāyaṇam) is a Sanskrit epic composed over a period of nearly a millennium, with scholars' estimates for the earliest stage of the text ranging from the 8th to 4th centuries BCE,[4] and later stages extending up to the 3rd century CE.[5] Ramayana is one of the two important epics of Hinduism, the other being the Mahābhārata.[6]
